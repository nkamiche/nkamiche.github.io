Overview

Welcome to the official Kangaroo Adventure Outfitters training documentation. This guide explains how to prepare for guided jumps, select the right gear, and stay safe while exploring Roo Ridge and the surrounding outback trails.

Required Equipment

Every participant should bring or rent the following items before a session:

  • Outback Trail Pack or Pouch Pack Rental
  • Closed-toe hiking shoes with ankle support
  • Joey Jump Training Kit balance board (for beginner sessions)
  • At least 1 liter of water per hour of activity
  • Outback Sun Hat and sunscreen

Warm-Up Routine

Complete this 10-minute warm-up before any jump training or trail hike:

  1. Five minutes of light marching or jogging in place
  2. Ankle circles and calf stretches (30 seconds each leg)
  3. Balance board practice with both feet (2 minutes)
  4. Practice hops in place with soft landings (10 repetitions)

Jump Technique

Proper form reduces strain and improves endurance on long outback routes. Bend your knees on landing, keep your chest upright, and use your arms for balance. Beginners should start on flat terrain before attempting Roo Ridge inclines with a guide.

Safety Checklist

  • Check weather conditions before departure
  • Confirm pack straps and hydration are secure
  • Stay within sight of your assigned guide
  • Report fatigue or discomfort immediately
  • Follow all posted trail markers and camp rules

Gear Maintenance

After each adventure, empty and air-dry packs, wipe down balance boards, and store equipment away from direct sunlight. Rental gear must be returned clean and dry to the Billabong Base Camp check-in desk.
